Kenneth A. Pickar
About Ken

Ken Pickar is retiring after teaching for 22 years at the California Institute of Technology, where he has been Visiting Professor and J. Stanley Johnson Professor of Mechanical Engineering. At Caltech he taught courses in Entrepreneurship, Management of Technology, Product Design for the Developing World and Design for Freedom from Disability. In 2005, he was chosen by Caltech undergraduate students for an Award for Excellence in Teaching.

Before Ken came to Caltech, he had a long career in corporate R&D and Engineering management. After finishing his PhD in Physics at Penn in 1966, he went to work at the famed Bell Laboratories in Murray Hill N.J. where he focused on semiconductor research. After 8 years at Bell, he went on a technology management journey that included successively senior roles at Bell Northern Research, Phillips Semiconductors, General Electric and AlliedSignal Aerospace (now Honeywell), which first brought him to Southern California.

Ken has held a number of board positions throughout his career, including at Albany Medical Center, Level One Corporation, Neustar Corporation, Ness Technologies and H2Scan Corporation. He was also on the Operating Committee of Idealab from 2004 to 2006. Since 1999, he has been a member of the Tech Coast Angels (Los Angeles) and has made early-stage investments through this Southern California Network in many startups.

Ken has also served on a number of non-profit Boards, including the LA Regional Food Bank, The Los Angeles Cleantech Incubator, the South Coast Botanical Garden and the Chamber Orchestra of the South Bay.

A New York City native, Ken attended the Bronx High School of Science and Queens College, City University of New York, where he graduated Phi Beta Kappa with degrees in Physics and Math. At the University of Pennsylvania, Ken graduated with a PhD in Physics where his research focused on the properties of superfluid liquid helium.
